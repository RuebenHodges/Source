After graduating from law school, Daniel served as a Special Assistant Wayne County Prosecutor in the county’s Juvenile Court. He continued serving Wayne County’s families and youth as an Assistant Deputy Director for the Wayne County Department of Children & Family Services.

Justice with Integrity

Commitment to Justice

As a private practitioner, Daniel represents litigants in the Wayne and Oakland County Juvenile and Probate Court systems. Daniel serves as the Secretary to the Wayne County Juvenile Trial Lawyers Association and is the past chair of the Oakland County Bar Association’s Juvenile Law Committee. Daniel formed Mercier Development LLC to realize a lifelong dream of redeveloping one of Detroit’s historic buildings into housing units, following in his family’s footsteps of creating housing for Wayne County residents. In 2003, Daniel realized his dream when he was awarded the bid to purchase Detroit’s historic Lafer Building; a nine–story building located on Broadway Street in downtown Detroit. He converted the building into housing units by creating a debt/equity financial package that was replicated by other downtown investors spurring the redevelopment of Detroit’s historical buildings over the past decade.
